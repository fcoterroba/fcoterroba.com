**What is the digital certificate?**

The digital certificate is an electronic certification issued by the [National Mint and Stamp Factory ‚Äì Royal Mint](https://www.sede.fnmt.gob.es/web/sede/inicio) that links its subscriber with signature verification data, thus confirming their identity. This certificate, also known as Citizen or User Certificate, is a digital document that contains your identification data. Thus allowing us to identify ourselves on the Internet and exchange information with other people and organizations with the guarantee that it's us.

The copy-paste is simple, but I'll summarize üòÇ

The digital certificate is an electronic certification issued by FNMT - RCM that serves us as a kind of "ID card" on the Internet. Having this we'll be able to do the vast majority of things for which, ordinarily, we would have to go to the City Council or competent body.

**Oh, really? What else can you do?**

Well, let me see, the digital certificate can be used for:

- Check our work history
- File tax returns
- Check DGT points
- Check where and since when you're registered
- Legally sign a PDF
- And a very long etcetera

In addition, every day there are more sites where you can perform bureaucratic procedures. And the best of all, is that almost everything is done instantly, without queues or waits.

**Come on, you've convinced me, how do I start?**

Although with the COVID-19 issue, this procedure is being slowed down but it's still working and you must start like this:

You enter the [following link](https://www.sede.fnmt.gob.es/certificados/persona-fisica/obtener-certificado-software), read the previous considerations and fill out the application via Internet (step 2).

The next step is to move away from the digital world and appear in person at a registration office. The day you propose to go to the office, (generally no appointment is necessary although it depends on the place) you must tell the security guard to notify to request a digital certificate, since, there usually isn't any queue or desk to get exclusively this type of certificates but they're opened as needed.

Once you're at the desk with the official, they'll search the government database for your file and ask you for the code you've received by email. That's the moment to give it to them. Then you can delete it because it won't be necessary anymore (still I tell you that I just in case still have it saved). Once you give it to them and go home, you'll have received an email with another code to finally download that file.

Watch out, you have to download the certificate on the same computer and with the same browser with which you made the application. Although later you'll be able to export the certificate to all browsers and/or computers you want.

Finally, the certificate will be downloaded and depending on the browser, it may even be installed.

**You said that in Google Chrome you can install the digital certificate, how is it done?**

Well, really, Google Chrome doesn't support certificates, but uses Internet Explorer's certificate store. Yes, it can be installed in Chrome, but let's talk with properties, above all üòÇ

What we must do is enter "Settings (icon with three vertical dots) > Type 'certificates' > Enter more". And then the option "Manage certificates" will appear, a window will open with all installed certificates and we'll click on the "Import" option.

Then a simple assistant will appear that will help us perform the installation.

![Chrome Certificate Import](https://fcoterroba.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Anotaci√≥n-2020-04-11-134717.png)
